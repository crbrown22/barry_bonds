*Barry Bonds

"A Tribute to The Greatest
Baseball Player of All Time"

"I want to be part of Major League Baseball's Hall of Fame, but I don't want to be part of the kind of Hall of Fame that's based on voters' beliefs and assumptions."

  • 14x All-Star
  • 12x Silver Slugger
  • 8x Gold Glover
  • 3x Hank Aaron Award Winner
  • 2x NL Homerun Leader
  • 1x NL RBI Leader
  • San Francisco Giants Retired No. 25

Batting Avg .298 / Home Runs 762 / Slugging % .607 / Stolen Bases 514

TIMELINE OF GREATNESS
  • 1985: Selected as the 5th pick in the MLB amatuer draft
  • 1986: Hits first career Homerun vs.Atlanta Braves
  • 1989: 19 HRs, 58 rbi's, and 14 outfield assist (2nd in MLB in assist).
  • 1990: Wins 1st MVP award .301 avg, 33 hr , 114 rbis, and 52 stolen bases.
  • 1990: Becomes a member of 30-30 Club, Awarded Gold Glove and Silver Slugger Awards
  • 1991: Gold Glove, Silver Slugger Award, finished 2nd in MVP votes
  • 1992: Wins 2nd MVP,.311 batting avg, 34 homeruns, 103 RBI's
  • 1993: Signs record deal of $43.75 million with the San Francisco Giants. Switches to #25 in honor of his father.
  • 1993: Wins 3rd MVP with .336 batting avg., 46 homerunes, and 123 RBI's
  • 1996: Becomes a member of the 40-40 Club.
  • 1996: 4th player in MLB history to acomplish a career total of 300 hr's and 300 stolen bases
  • 1997: Bonds has another 30-30 season with 43 HR's and 37 stolen bases
  • 1998: Hits 400th career HR, making Bonds the 1st player ever to make the 400-400 club
  • 1999: Ranked the best player in the 90's era
  • 2000: Bonds hits career highs .306 batting avg, .688 slugging %, 49 HR's, and 117 Walks
  • 2001: Hits 500th homerune, Breaks Mark MvGwire's single season homerun record with 73 HR's.
  • 2001: Wins 4th MVP and Awarded the Babe Ruth Homerun Award.
  • 2002: Bonds re-signs a 4 year, $90 million contract with San Franciso Giants
  • 2002: Wins 5th MVP, National League Batting Title,
  • 2002: Hits 600th career HR, .799 slugging % (4th highest all time), .582 on base %
  • 2003: Wins 6th MVP with 49 HR's, .341 battting avg., 500 career stolen bases becoming the only member of the 500-500 club
  • 2004: Bonds wins his 7th MVP batting .362, 45 HR's, .812 slugging %, and hits 700th career HR. Only player to have 4 consecutive MVP seasons.
  • 2005: Re-signs 2 year $22 million contract with San Francisco Giants. Spent most of the year with knee injury.
  • 2006: Bonds hits 715th homerun surpassing Babe Ruth in career homeruns and 734th homeruns passing Hank Aaron's National League record.
  • 2007: New York Daily News reports evidence leaks of Barry Bonds testing positive for amphetemines which had only been in effect for 1 season.
  • 2007: Bonds hits 756th homerun passing Hank Aaron for most career homeruns.
  • 2007: Barry Bonds retires after 22 seasons with 762 HR's, .298 batting avg., 2,835 Hits, 1996 RBI's, and 514 stolen bases.
